Experimental Documents Insights


The experimental documents provide the following insights about CXCL genes:

1. CXCL2: Significantly overexpressed in colorectal cancer, involved in tumor initiation, development, and angiogenesis. Knockdown reduces EMT markers and MMPs, inhibiting tumor proliferation and migration.
- Reference: Liu, Y. et al., 2020

2. CXCL9, CXCL10, and CXCL13: Associated with high densities of Th1/cytotoxic memory T cells and favorable prognosis in colorectal cancer. Involved in building an efficient immune contexture at the tumor site.
- Reference: Mlecnik, B. et al., 2011

3. CXCL16: Higher exodomain associated with aggressive ovarian cancer, promotes disease by CXCR6 activation and MMP modulation.
- Reference: Mir, H. et al., 2019

Conclusions for the BioinformaticsApplication Experiment


1. Overexpression of CXCL Genes: Several CXCL genes (e.g., CXCL1, CXCL3, CXCL8) show significant overexpression, indicating their potential role in promoting tumorigenesis and cancer progression.

2. Immune Response and Prognosis: CXCL9, CXCL10, and CXCL13 are linked to favorable prognosis due to their role in enhancing the immune response at the tumor site, suggesting their potential as biomarkers for immune contexture in cancer.
